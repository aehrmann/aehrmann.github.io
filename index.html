Pi-fi

Concept

This project is a creative exploration of the number Pi as data source for producing tones in the browser.

Technologies Used

  • HTML5 Web Audio API
  • AngularJS

How It Works

The project uses the digits of Pi as indices into a list of 10 frequency values, corresponding to the notes C4 - D4. As the digits are animated, the current digit is used to grab the frequency at that position in the list, which is then used to update the oscillator's frequency.

While the Web Audio API handles the production of the tones themselves, Angular manages the controls. Using two-way binding, the controller can update the status of the sounds (playing/paused), reset the playhead, and update the duration of the notes.

Future Plans

Right now, the project has several limitations, including a limited set of notes and the manner in which the notes are played. In future iterations, I plan to add a way to choose different sets of notes to play, most likely different scales and a greater range of octaves.

On the audio side, modifications to how the tones are actually produced could be improved. Right now, the simplest way to update the oscillator's frequency is to mute it, adjust the frequency, and then unmute. This has the side effect of making the notes sounds continuous. The goal for the next version would be to add a small gap of silence between the notes.

I also plan to add ways to select the format of the speed of the notes, adding an option to set the interval in the form of beats-per-minute, according to the way most audio applications work.

Another idea for an extension is the ability to loop a specific selection of notes. This would allow the user to investigate sub-patterns within the digits of Pi.

AptAmigo

Concept

As part of Startup Institute's partner project, I served as my team's project manager and assisted with the design and development of the project. Our partner, AptAmigo, is a Chicago-based startup, developing a platform for apartment rentals and condo purchases, providing real reviews from previous tenants.

Initial Direction

Dan Willenbourg, AptAmigo's founder, came to us with the initial goals of redesigning portions of the site to increase conversion rates, developing a customer relationship management system (CRM) to automate their then manual process, and develop and execute a marketing plan to propel AptAmigo forward.

For the first phase of the project, I worked with Robin McKay, a talented designer, researching effective landing page designs and usability improvements to the platform, in the hopes of decreasing bounce rates and increasing conversion rates.

The Pivot

About halfway through the project, AptAmigo's traffic had grown quickly and steadily enough that the bounce and conversion rates were no longer the issues they started as, and deisgn and development efforts shifted to making sure certain parts of the were as usable as possible to retain the new customers AptAmigo was attracting.

My team wanted to provide the same amoutn of value that we'd initially planned, so Robin and I shifted our focus to streamlining the content already on the site and presented functional protytpes of propsed modifications to its layout and minor tweaks to colors and typography.

Being on a tight deadline, and becuase of difficulty communicating with Dan's remote development team in Utah, I scraped the home page and one of the property listing pages so that I could develop and modify the designs. I also set up a Node.js-based static site on Heroku so that Dan and I could communicate about the changes I was making as close to real-time as possible.

The Result

Although the pivot changed the nature of the final deliverables for the project as a whole, we were able to provide Dan with a direction for design, producing tangible products in the form of functional and styled prototypes that the development team could use to update the production site.
